Try the activity → Read the pedagogy first
Section 01Try

An escape-room activity, before any theory.

Three checkpoints, three letter clues, one final challenge. The topic is deliberately low-stakes — Marvel character trivia — so the structure of the learning loop is what stands out, not the content. Wrong answers route through a reflection question so students can't lose, only learn.

Section 02Why

The constant feedback loop.

Most assessments end at the answer. This activity ends at the next question. Each decision opens a five-step loop that turns a wrong answer into two correct mental models — one for here, one for somewhere else.

Section 03Design

Three starter templates to design from.

Upload a thumbnail for each card, then link it to a template file your students can use to begin building their own decision-tree activity.

Section 04Build

Then build it with AI in the room.

When you're ready to draft, head to the companion builder. It scaffolds your stem questions, generates plausible wrong answers, and writes the where-this-answer-fits reframes that the loop depends on. You keep the pedagogical decisions; it handles the typing.

From blank page to a working decision tree in about an hour.

The builder asks you for a topic, three things you want students to actually understand, and the misconceptions you keep correcting in office hours. It returns a draft tree with feedback, reflections, and reframes — ready for you to edit, reshape, and ship.
